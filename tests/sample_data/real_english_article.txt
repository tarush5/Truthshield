India's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) grew by 8.2% in the fiscal year 2023-24, according to data released by the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation on May 31, 2024.

The growth was primarily driven by strong performance in manufacturing, construction, and services sectors. The agriculture sector recorded modest growth of 1.4% during the same period.

According to the Reserve Bank of India, inflation remained within the target band of 2-6%, averaging around 5.4% for the year. The central bank maintained its benchmark repo rate at 6.5% through most of the fiscal year.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman noted that the growth figures reflect the resilience of the Indian economy amid global uncertainties. The World Bank had projected India's GDP growth at 7.5% for the same period.

Sources: Ministry of Statistics (mospi.gov.in), Reserve Bank of India (rbi.org.in), Press Information Bureau (pib.gov.in)
